# GEOCON — The North-Star Question

> Recorded 2026-06-08 at the founder's request. This question is foundational:
> its answer is meant to anchor GEOCON's development from here. Edited and
> organized from the founder's own framing; keep the intent verbatim in spirit.

## The question

**If GEOCON were your own system — and your goal were to turn its mission into a
tool used at global scale — how would your strategy, design, and functionality
take shape?**

The mission, stated plainly: connect the *conservation* of threatened geophytes
to the *full chain of value* those plants can yield (academic, ecological,
chemical, translational, commercial) — while never letting commerce contaminate
conservation (the IUCN / non-profit firewall is structural, not cosmetic).

Answer it across every layer, and answer it **critically** — naming what is
wrong, confused, cluttered, or won't scale in the current code, design, and
concept before proposing what should replace it:

1. **Definition.** What *is* GEOCON, in one sentence? What category is it (or
   does it create)? How do you define it so a stranger — a person or an
   institution — understands it instantly?
2. **Strategy.** What is the wedge / beachhead to start from? What do you cut?
   What makes it defensible at global scale (data integrity, network effects,
   the firewall as an asset, a moat)?
3. **Design.** How should the product — its information architecture, its
   surfaces, its visual and interaction system — be shaped to serve that
   definition?
4. **Functionality.** What must it *do*, end to end? What is the core loop that
   creates and *proves* value at zero users and compounds with the network?
5. **User profiles.** Who are the real user types (undergraduate → professor,
   research assistant, public and private conservation bodies, industry R&D and
   production, funders, IPLC / traditional-knowledge holders, the curious
   public)? What does each *gain*? How do you expand these profiles and connect
   them to the system — and to each other?
6. **Usage.** How would the founder personally use the platform, day to day, to
   create and demonstrate value?
7. **Go-to-market — the most important part.** This is a genuinely new kind of
   system. How do you introduce it to people *and* to institutions? The
   narrative, the pitch, the first audiences, the proof, the naming and
   positioning. **Where do you start, and how do you define it so it lands?**

## Why this is recorded

The founder is deliberately slowing down to find direction before building
further. The answer to this question is the compass; everything built afterward
should be checkable against it.
