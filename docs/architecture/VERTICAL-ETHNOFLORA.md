# ETHNOFLORA — endemic medicinal-plant conservation vertical (design)

> Vertical 3 on BEE. GEOCON-parallel, firewall-separated, on the shared rails. NOT a geophyte clone.

**Recommended name:** ETHNOFLORA

**Name options:** ETHNOFLORA, VERA (Vernacular & Endemic Remedies Atlas), MATERIA, PHARMACOPOEIA GAP, PHAROS, RADIX, THERIAC, ENDEMICA MEDICA

## Mission
ETHNOFLORA is the open conservation atlas of what we do not yet know about saving the world's threatened endemic MEDICINAL plants — the species whose roots, bark, tubers, resins and whole bodies are harvested for human healing, and for which demand itself is the primary extinction driver. Its mission is to conserve threatened endemic medicinal plants and to connect that conservation to the full chain of value they yield — taxonomic, ecological, ethnobotanical, phytochemical, clinical, commercial — WITHOUT letting commerce contaminate conservation, AND without letting commerce extract traditional knowledge from the communities that hold it. The product is the gap: ~79% of assessed medicinal-and-aromatic-plant (MAP) species have NO conservation status, an even larger and more urgent unknown than the geophyte stubs. The one metric that matters is the same as GEOCON's — move one evidenced, provenance-labelled fact from 0 — but here the fact carries a second sacred dimension: a traditional-use claim that is attributed, consent-aware, and never auto-converted into a medical or commercial claim. Operator: VENN Bioventures OÜ (Estonia), parallel to GEOCON, on BEE.

## Why it differs from GEOCON
GEOCON's threat profile is habitat- and trade-driven and its conservation target (the bulb) is largely separable from the threat. ETHNOFLORA inverts this: for medicinal plants DEMAND IS THE EXTINCTION DRIVER and the harvested organ is frequently the root, rhizome, bark, tuber or whole plant — harvesting kills the individual (Goldenseal/Jatamansi roots, Prunus africana bark, Saussurea/Aconitum tubers). This forces three structural differences. (1) A THIRD knowledge axis GEOCON lacks — traditional/ethnobotanical use — which carries two hazards geophytes do not: medical-efficacy overstatement (an ethnobotanical 'used for X' is NOT a clinical claim) and traditional-knowledge appropriation (biopiracy). So ETHNOFLORA needs a SECOND one-directional firewall (knowledge/ABS), structurally identical to the money firewall but protecting communities: traditional knowledge flows in with attribution + consent preserved, and is never stripped of origin or auto-converted into a commercial claim. (2) 'Harvested part' and 'wild-collected vs cultivated supply' become CORE conservation columns (they determine whether use is a threat or a relief on wild populations) — money-free, conservation-side data that geophytes never needed. (3) A different reference backbone: re-anchor taxonomy on WCVP/POWO (CC-BY) and layer Kew MPNS for medicinal nomenclature + plant parts, IUCN MPSG for status, CITES/TRAFFIC for trade-legality COUNTS (not prices), FairWild for wild-harvest sustainability, and a TRAMIL-style ethnobotanical layer with a quantified significant-use threshold. The Venn engine, tic discipline, gap-as-product North-Star and the commerce firewall are reused unchanged; the integrity model gains one firewall and the data model gains three axes.

## Firewall model
TWO structural, one-directional firewalls, both enforced in Postgres (not policy). FIREWALL A — COMMERCE (reused from GEOCON exactly): zero money/price/revenue columns in conservation data; the bridge schema stays USAGE=service_role only; all ingress via _bridge_require_admin()-gated SECURITY DEFINER RPCs; the single door open_bridge_opportunity freezes a one-directional provenance snapshot into bridge.opportunities (vertical_id='medicinal_plants'); no FK from bridge back into conservation. FIREWALL B — KNOWLEDGE / ABS (NEW, ETHNOFLORA-specific, the originality): traditional-use facts are a sensitivity-tiered, consent-aware substrate. Every ethnobotanical_use_fact carries mandatory provenance: source community/region, Nagoya PIC/MAT status (consent_status enum: unknown | documented_public | pic_obtained | mat_in_place | restricted), benefit_sharing_ref, and a use-significance weight (TRAMIL-style >=20% citation-frequency threshold to count as 'significant use'). Two hard rules enforced in the door RPC: (1) open_bridge_opportunity REFUSES to open for any value output whose evidence lineage includes a traditional-knowledge fact lacking benefit-sharing provenance (consent_status in restricted/unknown) — the commerce door fails closed on ABS exactly as it fails closed on non-admin; the frozen snapshot is extended to carry abs_status so a downstream counterparty sees consent provenance. (2) A use-claim is never rendered as a clinical/medical claim: ethnobotanical use is provenance-tiered and labelled non-medical-advice at the schema and UI layer, inference capped at 0.5 exactly as GEOCON caps inferred evidence. Restricted/sacred knowledge is member/community-gated (extends GEOCON's existing locality sensitivity-tiering). Both firewalls point the same way: conservation/knowledge -> commerce, never back.

## Unique medicinal dimensions
- TRADITIONAL / ETHNOBOTANICAL USE as a first-class, sensitivity-tiered, consent-aware knowledge axis — with its OWN firewall (Firewall B / ABS), a TRAMIL-style >=20% significant-use weight, and a hard non-medical-advice label (ethnobotanical 'used for X' is never a clinical claim; inference capped at 0.5)
- NAGOYA / ABS PROVENANCE on every traditional-use fact — consent_status (unknown | documented_public | pic_obtained | mat_in_place | restricted), origin_community/region, benefit_sharing_ref; the commerce door fails closed when ABS provenance is missing
- HARVESTED PART as a core conservation column (root / rhizome / bark / tuber / whole plant / leaf / resin / seed) — because destructive vs non-destructive harvest of the medicinal organ is the central threat variable, unlike geophytes
- WILD-COLLECTED vs CULTIVATED SUPPLY as a core conservation column — the single variable (FairWild's reason to exist) that determines whether demand is a threat or a relief on wild populations; cultivation-substitution becomes a conservation OUTCOME type
- BIOACTIVE / PHYTOCHEMICAL COMPOUND profile (compound class, target bioactivity, plant part of occurrence) — richer than GEOCON's metabolites table; the value-chain vocabulary an industry licensing lead actually searches ('antioxidant bulb extract'), kept conservation-side and money-free
- HARVEST / WILDCRAFT PRESSURE as the PRIMARY extinction driver — a demand-driven threat model (overharvest of the medicinal organ) rather than GEOCON's habitat/trade-driven model; ~79% of assessed MAPs have no status (a larger, more urgent gap)
- CITES TRADE-LEGALITY + FAIRWILD SUSTAINABILITY status as conservation-side context (counts and certifications, never prices) — international legal exposure and sustainable-harvest baseline as part of the conservation record
- MEDICINAL NOMENCLATURE via MPNS — scientific<->pharmaceutical<->common names + plant parts tied to literature citations, a naming layer geophytes never needed
- COMMUNITY/INDIGENOUS-KNOWLEDGE-HOLDER as a credited contributor class — answering the Global-South critique: a 40-year traditional-knowledge holder must be attributable and credited (not extracted), with restricted/sacred knowledge community-gated

## Data sources
- **WCVP / POWO (Kew)** — Taxonomic spine — accepted names, synonymy, native distribution (TDWG WGSRPD) for all vascular plants; the backbone the new vertical re-anchors on (replacing geophyte-only lists). Used to seed real species, never fabricated. (OPEN, CC-BY 4.0, downloadable as a Darwin Core Archive via GBIF; R (kewr) / Python (wcvpy) tooling. Best free taxonomic backbone — ingest first.)
- **Kew MPNS (Medicinal Plant Names Services), V15 ~41,878 plants from 397 families** — The medicinal nomenclatural backbone: scientific names <-> pharmaceutical/drug names (incl. Chinese pinyin, Ayurvedic), common names, and PLANT PARTS, each tied to a literature citation. Inclusion = cited as medicinal in >=1 covered reference. Defines which species enter the vertical and the harvested-part vocabulary. (Free to SEARCH via portal (mpns.science.kew.org); NO public bulk download/API documented — Kew supplies tailored data subsets + a name-refresh web service BY ARRANGEMENT (mpns@kew.org). Treat as a licensing conversation, not an open download. [UNCERTAIN: exact V15 count from secondary summaries])
- **IUCN Red List + SSC Medicinal Plant Specialist Group (MPSG)** — Conservation status + the authoritative medicinal-conservation network. ~9% of assessed MAPs are threatened; ~79% have NO status (the gap = the product). MPSG drafted 60 N-American At-Risk assessments and reassessed ~400 European MAPs in 2024. (Red List = NON-COMMERCIAL-use terms (the existing IUCN/commerce firewall constraint applies identically — re-apply with 'No' to commercial use, as already planned for GEOCON). MPSG reports are public PDFs.)
- **CITES + CITES Trade Database (with TRAFFIC monitoring)** — International-trade LEGAL status for traded MAPs (~1,280 MAP species listed, >800 in Appendix II, against ~60,000 MAPs harvested globally). Flagships: Prunus africana, Nardostachys jatamansi, Saussurea costus, Boswellia, Dendrobium. Counts of LEGAL trade are money-free and fit the firewall. (Public listings + CITES Trade Database (counts, NOT prices — firewall-safe). Timing tailwind: World Wildlife Day 2026 is themed on medicinal & aromatic plants. [UNCERTAIN: counts shift each CoP])
- **FairWild Foundation (Standard v3.0, 2024)** — The wild-harvest SUSTAINABILITY frame (merger of fair-trade + ISSC-MAP ecological rigor; ~1.7M ha managed harvest areas, >12,000 harvesters in 2024). Provides the conservation-side 'wild vs cultivated' and sustainable-harvest baseline. CRITICAL: this is a conservation-side sustainability signal, NEVER a commerce hook. (Public standard (fairwild.org); third-party audited certification via Ecocert et al.)
- **TRAMIL (Caribbean ethnopharmacology network, since 1982)** — Methodological template for provenance-weighted traditional-use facts: documents which plant for which ailment with a quantitative SIGNIFICANT-USE threshold of >=20% citation frequency in participatory surveys; >90 plants in national pharmacopoeias. Anchors the ethnobotanical-use significance weight and the non-medical-advice discipline. (Public (tramil.net) + extensive peer-reviewed literature. A methodology to adopt, not just a dataset.)
- **Nagoya Protocol / CBD ABS (+ national ABS focal points)** — The legal/ethical regime for traditional knowledge + genetic resources: Prior Informed Consent (PIC) + Mutually Agreed Terms (MAT). A DESIGN CONSTRAINT, not a dataset — the schema attaches consent/origin metadata to every traditional-use fact and the commerce door refuses outputs lacking benefit-sharing provenance. (Public treaty text (cbd.int). Structural input to Firewall B, not an ingestible table.)
- **United Plant Savers At-Risk / To-Watch list** — NGO conservation layer naming concrete threatened N-American medicinal species (American Ginseng, Goldenseal, Blue Cohosh) — a real, citable seed list of harvest-pressured species for the strategic wedge. (Public (unitedplantsavers.org).)
- **GBIF occurrences** — Species-occurrence records (>1.6B) to fix spatial accuracy and bridge the status gap (Wikidata/GBIF), same upgrade path already recorded for GEOCON's globe. The DOI-citation credit-flow is also the template for making tics citable. (OPEN; Darwin Core; the WCVP DwC-A is distributed here.)

## Firewall risks
- ABS firewall is only as strong as its enforcement point: if open_bridge_opportunity does not actually WALK the full evidence lineage (only checks the top-level output), a value output derived from restricted traditional knowledge could leak through a child fact. The lineage walk must be exhaustive and fail-closed, and must be /crosscheck-verified.
- Non-medical-advice labelling can be bypassed at the UI/API layer if any surface (Ask, grant studio, public API) renders a traditional-use claim as efficacy. The non_medical_advice constraint must live at the schema + RPC level, not just in component copy.
- consent_status='unknown' is the dangerous default — if ingestion sets unknown silently, the door's fail-closed rule will block legitimate public-literature uses (documented_public) unless the ingestion correctly distinguishes them; mis-tagging in either direction is a real harm (over-block kills utility, under-block enables biopiracy).
- Same legacy-money-column risk as GEOCON: if any new medicinal market/price field is added to the conservation schema (anon-readable) it breaks Firewall A; keep ALL money in bridge.investors only, and physically evict any market_* columns rather than relying on revoked grants.
- Using IUCN/CITES conservation status as marketing surface on the Exchange floor (the GEOCON critique's 'narrative crossed the firewall' seam) is amplified for medicinal plants because CITES legal-trade data is commercially suggestive — keep trade COUNTS conservation-side and never let them imply an investable opportunity.
- Cross-vertical RPC leakage: until core species RPCs filter by vertical_id, a medicinal restricted-knowledge fact could surface in a geophyte query context; the vertical wrap (step 7) is a firewall dependency, not just UX.
- Community-gated/sacred knowledge tiering reuses GEOCON's locality sensitivity-tiering — if that RLS has any gap, sacred traditional knowledge inherits it; audit the tier RLS specifically before ingesting any restricted facts.
- Anticipation-of-listing motive leak (the conservation purist's critique) is sharper here: knowing a traditional-use fact could become a licensable output may bias whose knowledge gets recorded and how — enforce the structural lag (a fact cannot become listable until independently verified, and producers see no commerce signal inside the atlas).
