# v-next — the endemism vertical (11-agent brainstorm, answers to the founder questions)

> Ground (codebase + biogeography + multi-facet species) + 7 expert angles + synthesis. Conservation+value first, commerce last.

## Bottom line (recommendation)
Do NOT build an "endemic plants" vertical. Build endemism as a cross-cutting DIMENSION on the one shared atlas (an endemic_to relation at national/regional/point scale, provenance-labelled and versioned, surfaced as a public X/Y gear on every gearbox and a layer on the existing globe), and meet the founder's real second need — a home for the non-geophyte threatened/irreplaceable plants GEOCON structurally cannot hold — with ONE broad, scope-defined vertical (recommended name TOPOCON) that is NOT defined by endemism. The schema proves it on live data: vertical_id is text NOT NULL and 7,130 geophytes are already endemics inside GEOCON — a separate vertical would duplicate them or break the one-species-one-vertical invariant every RPC relies on. Country-specific orgs + invited foreigners are a SOCIAL layer built from existing primitives (region-scoped programs + the join door + steward/guest roles), with the steward's Z-consent gate coinciding with the equity gate and the money-blind receipt doubling as the ABS record. Multi-facet species (thyme/lavender) are one gearbox carrying all facets at once: conservation foregrounded structurally (gears near the hub, public), commerce money-blind at the rim — and because for these aromatics commerce is the very threat, harden the value-gear floor to a true lock when a wild-harvest flag is present. Keep it SIMPLE: the only new build is GATE-0 vertical-scoping (do it first, alone), a one-pass derivation of endemic from native_countries (turns 3 -> 7,130 endemics with zero ingestion, lighting the dimension up on the existing heat layer), and the broad-vertical row. Sequence: GATE-0 -> close ONE real evidenced loop in the existing second vertical first -> then open the broad vertical. Mission: "prove the plant that belongs to one place before it is lost." Metric: a local researcher minting an evidenced receipt for their own country's endemic, from 0.

## 1. Is endemism the right axis?
YES — endemism is the right organizing AXIS, but it is a dimension, not a vertical. It is the most inclusive, best-evidenced conservation axis available: it captures threatened + non-threatened + undiscovered in one honest net, and it runs on data you actually have. Hard numbers confirmed live: 7,130 of 47,052 geophytes are single-country natives (the endemism signal is already in the atlas via native_countries), versus only ~3,276 species with real IUCN status. So endemism reaches vastly more species than threat does. It is also intrinsically a conservation framing — it is literally the IUCN/Conservation-International hotspot entry criterion (>=1,500 endemic plants + >=70% habitat lost) — so it reinforces the firewall rather than straining it. The one caution: endemism is a RELATION between a species and a place, not a property of the species alone, and it is a moving target (>1 new endemic species/week in Turkey; constant splits/lumps). So every endemism claim must be provenance-labelled and versioned, never stored as fixed fact.

## 2. Separate vertical vs integrate as a dimension
INTEGRATE as a cross-cutting dimension — decisively. This is the single most important finding and six of seven lenses converged on it. The schema forces it: species.vertical_id is text NOT NULL (confirmed live) — a single-valued, mandatory partition, one species in exactly one vertical. A vertical answers WHAT KIND of plant this is (geophyte = a morphological class). Endemism answers WHERE it lives and nowhere else (a relation). You cannot model an attribute as a partition. The decisive number: 7,130 geophytes are ALREADY endemics living inside GEOCON right now. A separate endemic vertical would force every Crocus-type species (geophyte AND Turkish endemic) into two cabinets at once — which the schema physically forbids — or duplicate the row (two gearboxes, two program sets, two receipts, the multi-facet nightmare), or make vertical_id an array (which detonates every read RPC, fetchSpeciesPage, the gearbox aggregation, program ownership, receipt lineage, VerticalSwitcher — exactly the complexity that killed the personalization arc). HYBRID is the precise shape: endemism is a DIMENSION in data (an endemic_to relation on the shared species row, at three scales: national / regional-hotspot / point-endemic, each provenance-labelled), rendered as a public X/Y GEAR on every gearbox; and the founder's real second need — a home for the NON-GEOPHYTE plants GEOCON structurally cannot hold (Thymus, Salvia, Sideritis, Origanum, Lavandula are not geophytes) — is met by ONE broad, scope-defined vertical, NOT named or defined by endemism. Endemism is then the lens laid across GEOCON, the broad vertical, and every future vertical alike.

## 3. Country orgs + foreign researchers (sovereignty/ABS)
The founder's "country-specific orgs + invited foreign researchers" is a SOCIAL/organizing need, not a data-model need — and the key finding is sovereignty-shaped: an endemic species is, by definition, somewhere's heritage and nowhere else's, which gives a cold-start platform its strongest recruitment hook (a Turkish or Iranian botanist shows up for "the 3,650 plants that exist only in Turkiye" in a way morphology never delivers). Model it in THREE layers, none of which needs a new vertical: (1) AXIS = the endemic_to dimension + the existing globe heat layer; (2) GEOGRAPHY = "areas of endemism" as first-class scientific units citing the literature (Zagros — 1,167 endemics, 746 exclusive; West-Taurus; Anatolian Diagonal; Alborz; Mediterranean; Cape) — neutral, sourced, never named after a geopolitical claim you cannot adjudicate; (3) COMMUNITY = a region-scoped program cluster + the EXISTING join door (request_to_join_program / respond_to_join_request). Critical anti-colonial finding (verified): neither Iran nor Turkiye is a party to the Nagoya Protocol — so there is no ready ABS permit pathway; this is an OPPORTUNITY, not a blocker — the platform's own consent+attribution architecture becomes the trust layer and the LOCAL institution holds the door while the foreigner is the guest. Two roles: STEWARD (in-country, can open work, admit collaborators, and holds the consent key on any Z/commercial branch on a national endemic) and GUEST (invited foreigner, contributes X/Y for credit, cannot unilaterally open a Z branch). The elegant part that reconciles with v-next: the consent gate and the equity gate are the SAME gate — equity is born only at a Z output, which is exactly the moment national-heritage consent is required; the money-blind Provenance Receipt (get_chain_receipt) already records who contributed and consented, so it doubles as a de-facto ABS/benefit-sharing ledger with almost no new code. Cold-start rule: do NOT auto-launch chapters from data — a region goes public only once a real in-country steward claims it (recruit steward first, open chapter second).

## 4. Multi-facet species (thyme/lavender)
Do NOT file the species under one facet — the facets are independent axes carried simultaneously, and in the gear model the species is a GEARBOX where each facet is a GEAR. Thyme/oregano/lavender/Crocus each carry {endemic-to-X} + {threat-status} + {Z-potential} at once on one shared species row in their home vertical. Conservation is foregrounded STRUCTURALLY, not by editorial taste: the endemism gear and the threat/safeguard gears sit near the hub, are public, and turn first; the Z value gear sits at the rim, is money-blind, Exchange-only, and physically cannot turn until the conservation gears settle. The evidence makes this non-negotiable rather than aesthetic: for these aromatics the COMMERCIAL VALUE IS THE CONSERVATION THREAT — wild over-harvest for essential-oil/carvacrol demand is the documented extinction driver pushing Origanum minutiflorum (78.6% carvacrol, in Turkiye's top-10 threatened MAPs) toward extinction; "the majority of exported herbs are harvested from wild sources." So the mission for such a gearbox is "protect the wild endemic range + propagate ex-situ so demand is met by farms not by digging out the last wild plants" (X/Y, public, foregrounded), while carvacrol's real ChEMBL Phase-2 bioactivity is recorded ONLY as a money-blind Z potential (a documented potential, never a product, never a price). The firewall is intact and in fact PROVEN by these cases: zero money columns in conservation data, value_json money-blind CHECK, money-blind receipt; commerce may only cite, one-directional. One concrete hardening recommended: for species carrying a wild-harvest-pressure flag (CITES + documented trade signal), escalate the value-gear floor from the current soft cap-toward-zero to a TRUE LOCK — the Z fan is withheld, not just dimmed, until a non-wild-supply safeguard tooth is settled, and the T1 potential fan is member-gated (not world-public) so the atlas never publishes a treasure-map to the last wild populations. One conditional, no new subsystem.

## The mission + one metric
Mission (sharper than GEOCON's, same backbone): "Prove the plant that belongs to one place and exists nowhere else — before it is lost." An endemic's loss is not local but global: a place's living signature erased from Earth. That irreversibility frame is a stronger emotional hook than morphology and fits the North-Star ("the gap is the product") perfectly — a newly-described endemic is a brand-new gearbox with every gear empty, the purest "fact from 0." THE ONE IMPACT METRIC: an evidenced fact (DOI -> money-blind receipt) minted for an endemic species BY A RESEARCHER FROM THE COUNTRY THAT SPECIES IS ENDEMIC TO — i.e. "local researcher x own endemic x evidenced receipt," moved from 0. This is GEOCON's "evidenced fact from 0" given local OWNERSHIP. Commerce is never in the metric, never the headline; conservation IS the value (X/Y outputs are the impact), and equity exists only if a Z output is ever commercialized in the Exchange.

## Cold-start wedge
Launch on ONE area of endemism, not "all endemics globally" (~10-15% of vascular flora = too big/heterogeneous). WEDGE: the Irano-Anatolian corridor (Turkiye + Iran), starting even narrower at a single area — West-Taurus (one of the 4 Turkish centers holding 59% of the country's endemics in 16% of its area), then expand to Zagros (Iran, 1,167 endemics). Why this wedge: (1) founder's home advantage — Turkish language, local network, cultural/geographic proximity to Iran (the only real asset a 0-user platform has); (2) the densest temperate endemism wedge on Earth — Turkiye ~3,650 endemics (31%, meeting-point of 3 hotspots), Iran 2,597 endemics (32%, ~75% mountain-restricted); (3) gap-as-product is live — >1 new species/week in Turkiye, most Data Deficient; (4) complements GEOCON without competing — Mediterranean is the bulb heartland (overlap) but thyme/sage/Sideritis are non-geophytes GEOCON structurally cannot hold (the gap the broad vertical fills). HOW IT BOOTSTRAPS FROM 0: founder mints the first evidenced receipt himself for a West-Taurus endemic (the single-player verb proven first) -> that closed loop becomes an "invite card" -> a real in-country STEWARD claims the region and the chapter goes public -> a foreign Crocus/endemics specialist is invited in for CREDIT (not money) via the existing join door. Verb first, community second; credit + ownership are the currency a cold-start can actually offer.

## Reconciliation (THE BOOK / gear / firewall / equity)
Sits cleanly on the locked v-next with no model changes. THE BOOK: the atom is a POSITION (researcher x species x work-area); endemism/range-delimitation is a WORK-AREA a researcher takes a position on — exactly the atom — not a class of species, so it belongs on the species axis as a gear, not as a new atlas. RADIAL GEAR MODEL: the species page stays the GEARBOX; endemism becomes one more public branch rendered from the species centre (X-safeguard: define/protect range, EOO/AOO, ex-situ backup; Y-knowledge: cross-border conservation synthesis), running on the existing GENERIC Venn engine — no engine change. Evidence still enters only via the verb (DOI fact -> money-blind receipt); the 'endemic is a moving target' caution is a FEATURE here — each claim is provenance-labelled, versioned, minted as a receipt, which is the gap-as-product working. FIREWALL: endemism sits on the X/Y side of the radius and is the IUCN hotspot criterion itself, so it reinforces the firewall (commerce may only cite, one-directional, money-blind at write/store/read). EQUITY-AT-Z: unchanged and sharpened — the home-country steward's consent gate IS the equity gate (both fire only at a Z output), and the receipt lineage credits conservation contributors (credit-in-lineage, never cash) while equity accrues only to the Exchange actor. ETHNOFLORA FOLDS IN: 'medicinal use' was the same category error — a property (ethnobotany + Nagoya/ABS = Firewall B) carved into a class slot. Retire medicinal_plants as a vertical (never delete: migrate its 361 rows into the broad vertical, preserving vertical_attrs provenance) and promote its two real ideas — ethnobotany and ABS-consent — to reusable cross-cutting GEARS on the same rail as endemism. Net: one broad vertical + four cross-cutting dimensions (endemism, threat, ethnobotany, ABS-consent) that GEOCON and every vertical share. Strictly simpler and it generalizes.

## Name candidates (GEOCON-style)
- **TOPOCON** — True sibling of GEOCON: same -CON (conservation) spine, swapping geo (earth/form) for topos (place). Reads 'conservation atlas organized by place' — broad, inclusive, conservation-first, zero commerce scent, pronounces cleanly in Turkish. Names the BROAD non-geophyte vertical without uttering 'endemic' (keeping endemism free as the cross-cutting gear). One TM check: 'Topcon' optics brand is one letter off — verify before committing. Strongest overall.
- **CHORACON** — Most scientifically precise: chora/choros is the literal root of biogeographic distribution science, and the founder's real unit (an 'area of endemism' spanning borders — Zagros, the Anatolian Diagonal) IS a chora. Conservation-first via -CON. Cost: low lay recognition, must teach itself. Pick if rigor beats instant legibility.
- **CHOROS** — Clean-break alternative if you want the two verticals to read as siblings rather than 'GEOCON and GEOCON-2'. Abstract place-word, serious, visually weighty like GEOCON, non-commercial. Weaker only because nothing in it explicitly says 'conservation' (left implied by the shared atlas).
- **REFUGIA** — Scientific term for glacial refuges where lineages persist; emotional + geographic, overlaps 'areas of endemism', conservation-true. Good if the founder wants the dimension/community word to evoke survival-of-place rather than a -CON coinage.
- **ENDEM** — Shortest, GEOCON-parallel, universally legible root; works as the plain DIMENSION/gear label or a community handle. CAUTION: do NOT use it to name the broad vertical — that re-commits the ETHNOFLORA error of carving a property into a class slot and excludes the non-endemic-but-threatened plants the founder explicitly wants. Best reserved as the lens label, not the vertical brand.
